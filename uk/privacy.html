Tilt Privacy Policy

Effective: 06 July 2015

Introduction

This policy explains how, together with the options you have when you submit data or create or configure a Tilt Page, we collect, store, use, share and protect your personal information. It also lays out the choices and rights you have with respect to this information. This is our Privacy Policy.

This Privacy Policy applies to our Services (see the Terms of Use for a definition), regardless of how you access them. By using Tilt.com or any of our Services, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you don’t agree, please don’t use Tilt.

Information we Collect

When you use our Services, we collect certain information about you and your contacts. The particular information we collect varies depending on how you use our Services, the options you select, and whether you are an Organiser or a Contributor.

Information You Give Us

We collect, store, and use information about you and your contacts that you give us when you use our Services. This includes:

  • Contact information, like your name, email address, phone number, mailing address, and your city, state, and country of residence.
  • Financial information, like your bank account numbers or payment card numbers and billing address.
  • Additional personal information, like your date of birth or government issued identification number. We only request this information under certain circumstances and as permitted by law.
  • Your username and password.
  • Information about your access and use of and interaction with our Services.
  • Information sent to us by the device(s) you use to access our Services, including data on the pages you access, your location, mobile network information, device type, device ID, standard web log data, and your Internet protocol (IP) address. Although this information is generally non-identifying, where it can be used to identify you we treat it subject to the same safeguards that we apply to personal information.
  • Your email address book if you invite your email contacts to try Tilt or your Facebook friends list when you import them from Facebook.
  • Other information that you provide to us, like email messages, posts to Tilt Pages, responses in forms, or other information sent to us through the Services.

Where you provide information to us about a third party, such as your friends, you must obtain the consent of that person to the use of their information in accordance with this Privacy Policy before you provide it to us.

Information Sent to Us by Third Parties

You may log into our Services using Facebook Connect or other similar third-party applications. These applications authenticate your identity and provide you the option to share certain personal information with us about yourself and others, which varies according to the service you use or the option you select. We collect, store, and use this information as described in this Privacy Policy.

We may also collect information about you from other sources as permitted by law, such as credit bureaus, identity verification services, or our payment processors, and combine that with information we collect on our Services.

Why We Collect This Information

We may use your personal information to:

  • Provide you with access to and use of our Services.
  • Allow Organisers to promote Pages both on and off the platform.
  • Ensure our records are accurate, complete, and verified by third parties.
  • Give you awesome customer support.
  • Process transactions and send you notices about them.
  • Customize, measure, and improve our Services.
  • Resolve disputes, troubleshoot problems, and collect fees.
  • Prevent, detect, and investigate potentially prohibited, illegal or fraudulent activities.
  • Enforce the Tilt Terms of Use, Community Guidelines, Organiser Guidelines, Contributor Guidelines, Acceptable Use Policy and our other policies.
  • Deliver targeted service update notices, promotional offers, marketing and Tilt Page updates, based on your communication preferences (email, phone, SMS, etc.).
  • Enable you to invite your friends to try Tilt via various means (such as text message, email and otherwise), if you’d like.
  • So Tilt and Organiser’s can send you things in the mail like promotional materials, rewards, or purchases.

Where you provide information about a third party (such as your friends) to us, you must obtain the consent of that person to the use of their information in accordance with this Privacy Policy before you provide it to us.

How We Share This Information

Information Shared on the Services

Our services are social and community focused, which means some of your activities on the Services will be visible to other Users as well as more broadly on the internet. The extent to which your information is shared depends on whether you are the Organiser of or the Contributor to a public or private Page. Generally speaking, expect anyone to be able to see you started or contributed to a public Page and except some level of degree of discretion from private Pages (though it is important to know there are ways that anyone can see a private Page).

See our Public v. Private FAQ to learn more.

Information Shared with Organisers

If you contribute to a Tilt Page, we may share certain personal information about you with the Organiser, including your name, email address, mailing address, the amount of your contribution and order specifications. We share partial payment information with Tilt Organisers, including the last four digits of your payment card number, so that they can do things like process and fulfill your order, process refunds, and dispute chargebacks.

Information Shared with Third Parties

We may share your personal information and/or financial information with:

  • Payment service providers or banking partners in order to facilitate your financial transactions or as required by credit card association rules.
  • Credit bureaus and collection agencies to report account information, as permitted by law.
  • Verified nonprofit organizations so that they can send you a receipt for your tax-deductible contribution.
  • Affiliates or other trusted third parties that process personal information for us or help with our business operations for the purposes set out above, such as marketing, fraud prevention, compliance, and technology services. Such affiliates or trusted third parties will be subject to obligations to process such information in compliance with the same safeguards that we deploy and all such disclosures will only be made in accordance with applicable laws.
  • Other third parties when we believe it is reasonably necessary to comply with the law or a regulator, protect any person from death or serious bodily injury, prevent fraud or abuse of Tilt or our users, or protect Tilt's property rights. If we’re going to release your information in response to legal process, our policy is to provide reasonable advance notice, along with a copy of the request, unless prohibited from doing so by law or court order. Where prior notice is prohibited, our policy is to notify affected users after the non-disclosure period has lapsed. We may disclose information without prior notice if we believe it’s necessary to prevent imminent and serious bodily harm to a person or other criminal or illegal acts. Nothing in this Privacy Policy is intended to limit any legal objections or defenses our users may have to efforts by third parties to compel disclosure of information, including legal orders from the government.
  • Companies we plan to merge with or to be acquired by. In the event that Tilt (or a part thereof) is (i) subject to negotiations for the sale of all or any part of its business or assets or (ii) is sold to a third party or undergoes a re-organisation, you agree that any of your personal information which it holds may be disclosed to such third party (or its advisors) as part of any due diligence process or transferred to that re-organized entity or third party and used for the same purposes or for the purpose of analyzing any proposed sale or re-organisation.
  • Other third parties with your consent.

As noted above, Tilt will share information with Organisers for Pages. Tilt will not otherwise provide any of your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes except with your prior consent. For example, we may from time to time offer co-branded experiences with other companies on the Services, and if we or they obtain your consent for those companies to receive and use your information for purposes identified in the consent, then we will share your information with them.

We reserve the right to anonymize your personal data and may share aggregated, non-personally identifiable information publicly and with our partners, including publishers, advertisers, or affiliated sites. For example, we may share information with others to show trends about the general use of the Services.

Promotional Uses

We may publically feature your Page if it is public (but not if its private) and contributions to that Page on an examples like Page on the Services, or other similar pages. We may publically feature your Tilt Page or you as a customer on various Tilt pages and in marketing and promotional materials, including those trademarks you use on our Services.

Use Outside the European Economic Area

Your personal information may be accessed by staff or suppliers in, transferred to, and/or stored at, a destination outside of your country of residence in which data protection laws may be of a lower standard than your country of residence. Regardless of location or whether the person is an employee or contractor we will impose the same data protection safeguards that we deploy inside the European Economic Area.

Your Privacy Preferences

Tilt lets you set your preferences on the Services for the use and sharing of your information, including how or when we and Organisers contact you.

If you don’t want to receive newsletters, Page updates, or marketing communications from us or participate in our ad-customized programs, simply indicate so in your settings panel on the relevant Services or unsubscribe here.

We use cookies as set out in our Cookies Policy and any personal information obtained from such cookies in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

How We Protect Your Information

We and our cloud provider protect your information using physical, technical, and administrative security measures to reduce the risks of loss, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, and alteration. Safeguards include firewalls and data encryption, physical access controls to the data centers, and information access authorization controls.

When you enter financial information (such as a payment card number or your bank account information) on our Services, we encrypt the transmission of that information using secure socket layer technology (SSL).

We collect, store and transmit cardholder data under the most stringent credit card security standard: PCI Compliance Level 1. A PCI-certified auditor verified and awarded us this level of security and compliance.

We work hard to secure your data, but nobody is perfect. If you discover a vulnerability in our Services, please email security@tilt.com and we’ll look into it. We ask that you don’t publicly discuss the vulnerability until Tilt has had an opportunity to address it.
